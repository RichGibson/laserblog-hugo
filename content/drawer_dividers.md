I wanted to make drawer dividers for my tool boxes, especially the out of control screwdriver
drawer. I did a little search, and decided that it was worth it to just 
pay $3 for an SVG of [drawer dividers](https://www.etsy.com/shop/Pacificgrovedesigns) from 
Pacific Grove Designs on Etsy.

The pattern is designed for 1/8" material, so I will need to scale up for 1/4"

I almost feel like I should just take inspiration from the picture and design my own
version.

A parameteric version would be cool. How long do you want your pieces? But I can just cut
them to length.

(Later) I did 'design my own,' ultimately mostly doing it in LaserCut 5.3, which is close
to the worst tool there is...

I used CAD (cardboard aided design), and then I was in the room but not paying attention
and I had a fire in the laser cutting cardboard at too high a power.

And now the focusing/spotting laser is not working. I am hoping that a wire was
burned, or something easy.

It is an opportunity to 'earn' my laser through sweat equity. I mean, I own it, but you
don't own a tool until you have bled, or at least sweat, a bit for it!
