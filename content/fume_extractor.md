My blower has stopped working. I took it apart, and I don't know how to fix it.

I found a data plate, but everything was in Chinese. Google Translate looked at
it and told me it was 240 m^3 per hour, which is about 140 CFM.

I have 6" ducting.

I ordered this from Rabbit Laser
https://www.rabbitlaserusa.com/product/Vacuum-Blower-Fan-370W-220VAC-6-Inch-Flange

440 CFM. I can try my charcol pads again - that I tried to use but they blocked
too much airflow.




## Original Date: 2024-07-31 10:00
Zach [wrote an instructable](https://www.instructables.com/Build-a-laser-cutter-fume-extractor/) about his DIY Fume     Extractor.

Lensdigital [Wrote about his build](https://www.lensdigital.com/home/diy-fume-extractor-for-laser-cutter/).

And now perhaps it is my turn.

But it is a lot of work, and how well does it work?

I don't like the carbon being on the sides, it means that if the carbon isn't completely full
the fumes go around it. would prefer carbon to be horizontal.


<img src="/images/fume_extractor_blower.jpg" alt="The blower with the hose off" width=200>

You can see the little squirrel in there. Or maybe just the squirrel cage blower.

![Cutting a circle](/images/fume_extractor_cut_circle.jpg)

I assumed I would do a dog's breakfast of a job cutting a circle, but I 
was able to use the blower hole as a template.


![Wire to hold it in place](images/fume_extractor_wire.jpg)

I started with three holes to thread the wire through. That seemed like enough, and 
I wanted less wire on the outside so that the vent hose would still fit.

Well, if you go in and out of three holes you end up with the ends of the wire
on the outside, where they will interfere even more! So a fourth hole, and a 
totally non-symmetrical finish.

So how does it work? I am not sure. A lot of smell comes out when you open the lid,
but not as much while cutting.
