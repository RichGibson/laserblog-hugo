I finally got my GigaMacro rig up to the 'new' house (we have been here 
for 17 months, so 'new' seems a bit of an over statement).

It is in a sub-nominal condition, there is rust, and wear from neglect. But 
it is, to me, a beautiful object, and I would like it functional again!

I want to set it up to be able to enjoy it, and also to possibly use it. Taking
high resolution images used to be something I valued! 

but I need a table. 

I watched some videos, ([This one from Bear Mountain Builds](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3SQlaoUbQDo) was helpful), and then I ordered another set of the casters that are working on another project.

And I dug into my deeply atrophied [OpenSCAD](https://openscad.org) skills
and [made a model](static_files/macro_rig_table.scad).

I downloaded a STEP format model from McMaster Carr for similar castors to the ones I will use, 
and then used an [online STEP to STL conversion tool](https://polyd.com/en/online-step-to-stl-converter).

This is a low-laser project...but maybe I can etch designs onto the wood or something.
